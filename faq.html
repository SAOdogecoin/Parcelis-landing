Everything you need to know about Parcelis — how it works, what it covers, and how to get started.

About Parcelis
What is Parcelis?
Parcelis is a shipping insurance platform for e-commerce merchants. It adds a protection option at checkout — customers opt in, pay the premium, and file claims directly with Parcelis if something goes wrong. You never touch a claim.
How is Parcelis different from carrier insurance?
Carrier insurance requires you to file the claim and wait 30–60 days. Parcelis puts the customer in control — they file directly through our portal, professional adjusters handle the review, and claims close in 5–7 days. No merchant involvement, no chasing carriers.
Who is InsureShip and why does it matter?
InsureShip is the licensed insurance carrier behind Parcelis. They hold the DOI licenses across all operating states. That means Parcelis protection is real, regulated insurance — underwritten and compliant — not a store credit policy or tech workaround.
Is this real insurance or a tech workaround?
Real insurance. Parcelis is powered by InsureShip and licensed in all operating states. Full DOI oversight, not a tech workaround.
Getting Started
How do I get started with Parcelis?
One-click install from the Shopify App Store. Plugins also available for WooCommerce, BigCommerce, Magento, and NEXT Commerce. A custom API is available for headless and bespoke storefronts. Protection appears at checkout automatically after install — no configuration required.
Is there a long-term contract required?
No. Parcelis is month-to-month with no lock-in. No setup fees, no minimums, no cancellation penalties.
Coverage & Protection
What does Parcelis coverage include?
Lost packages during transit, damaged items in shipping, and stolen packages — including porch piracy. Coverage applies across all major domestic and international carriers with no geographic limitations.
Does Parcelis cover porch piracy and package theft?
Yes. Packages marked as delivered but subsequently stolen are covered. The customer files a claim through the Parcelis portal the same way as any other loss — no police report required in most cases.
Which carriers are supported?
All major carriers — USPS, UPS, FedEx, DHL — plus regional and international carriers. Coverage is detected automatically at checkout based on the shipping method selected.
What are the coverage limits?
Up to $2,500 per box and $25,000 per shipment. For orders above these limits, contact us about enterprise coverage options.
What's NOT covered by Parcelis?
Standard exclusions include: buyer's remorse and returns, prohibited or restricted items, incorrect address provided by the customer, and packages with falsely declared value. Full exclusions list is included in the policy documentation.
Can I insure international shipments?
Yes. Parcelis covers international orders with no geographic limitations. Coverage is applied automatically at checkout when an international shipping method is selected.
Pricing & Costs
How much does Parcelis cost?
Free to install. No setup fee, no monthly minimum. Customers pay the protection premium at checkout — you keep a share on every protected order.
Who pays for the protection — me or my customers?
The customer pays the protection premium at checkout when they opt in. Merchants pay nothing. You earn a revenue share on every protected order placed.
Are there any setup fees or monthly minimums?
No. There are no setup fees, no monthly minimums, and no hidden charges. Parcelis is entirely free to install and operate — your only costs are zero.
Claims & Process
How fast are claims paid?
5–7 business days on average. Claims are filed directly by the customer through the Parcelis portal and handled by professional adjusters end-to-end.
How does the customer file a claim?
Through a branded claims portal hosted by Parcelis. It takes under 2 minutes to submit. Carrier data is pulled automatically — customers don't need to chase documentation. No merchant involvement required.
Do I need to do anything when a claim is filed?
No. Customers file directly through our portal. Parcelis handles resolution end-to-end — you get a notification when it's closed.
